Why one word a day works

It's not about cramming. It's about consistency.

Research shows that spaced repetition ‚Äî learning small amounts regularly over time ‚Äî is far more effective than intensive study sessions. Your brain consolidates memories during sleep, so learning one word each morning gives it 24 hours to stick.

Active recall (writing your own sentence) strengthens neural pathways more than passive reading. That's why we ask you to use each word, not just read its definition.
